Key Authorities

Key authorities identify the institutions that shape, administer or influence IP protection in Sweden. This section matters because Swedish IP protection operates through an interaction between national administration, EU-wide registration systems and international filing frameworks rather than through a purely domestic authority model.

National Authority Patent- och registreringsverket (PRV)
Official English Name Swedish Intellectual Property Office
Primary Role Core Swedish public authority for intellectual property administration concerning patents, trademarks and designs, with a broader national mission in relation to intellectual property rights and awareness.
Responsibilities Processes national applications, maintains relevant registers, provides IP information and supports understanding of patents, trademarks, designs and copyright in the Swedish context.
Typical Interaction Businesses interact with PRV when seeking Swedish national protection, reviewing filing options, checking registrability questions or clarifying how Swedish IP administration works in practice.
Official Website prv.se/en
Cross-Border Relevance Important for Swedish national rights and for coordination between Swedish filings, EU rights and international protection strategies.
EU Authority European Union Intellectual Property Office (EUIPO)
EU Function Administers European Union trade marks and Community designs, which are often commercially relevant for businesses operating in Sweden because one EU registration may cover the Swedish market together with the rest of the EU.
Typical Swedish Relevance A business active in Sweden may choose an EU trade mark or Community design route instead of, or in addition to, a Swedish national filing, depending on territorial scope and enforcement priorities.
EU Website euipo.europa.eu
European Patent Layer Patent protection for Sweden often interacts with the European patent system rather than only with a purely national filing logic, which means patent planning may involve both Swedish and broader European routes.
International Institutions / Filing Systems World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) filing systems are relevant where businesses use international mechanisms for patent, trademark or design expansion beyond Sweden and the EU.
Practical International Relevance A Swedish or foreign rights holder may begin with Swedish review, continue through an EU route for market coverage, and then extend protection internationally where manufacturing, licensing or export exposure requires broader territorial control.
PRV states that four principal IP right types are patent, trademark, design and copyright, that copyright arises automatically, and that the other three may be protected through PRV. This makes PRV the central domestic administrative authority, while EUIPO and international filing systems become relevant when Swedish market protection is part of a wider territorial strategy.
